Elephants are large animals of the family. Elephants and the order proboscidea. Two species are traditionally recognised, the African elephant (loxodonta africana) and tha Asian elephant (Elephant maximum). Elephants are scatterered throughout subsaharan Africa, South Asian, and Southeast Asia. Male African African Elephants are the largest extant terrestrial animals and can reach a height of 4m (13ft) and weigh 7000kg (15000lb).

All elephants have several distinctive features the most notable of which is a long trunk or proboscis, used for many purposes, particularly breathing, lifting water and graspung objects. Their incisors grow into trusks, which can sere as weapons and as tools for moving objects and diging. their piller like legs can carry their great weight. African elephants have larger earsand concave backs. wWhile Asian elephants have smaller ears and convex or level backs.

Elephants are herbivorous and can be found in different habitants including savannahs, forests, deserts and marshes. they prefer to stay near water.
